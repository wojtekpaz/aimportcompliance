FAQ

What brokers ask first.

Does the model ever invent a code that isn't in the tariff?

No. At each branch the engine hands the model the candidate headings it pulled from the CN and TARIC tables, and the model picks one or asks for a fact. A code outside that list cannot reach the result, because the model never writes the digits itself.

What happens when my description leaves out a deciding fact?

The engine stops at the branch where the facts run out and asks one question, the same question a classifier would ask: knitted or woven, steel or aluminium. You answer, and the reasoning continues from that branch instead of starting over.

Can I override the engine at a branch?

Yes. Each branch shows the candidates the engine weighed and the rule it applied. Pick a different candidate and the trail records your choice, then the reasoning continues from there. You stay the declarant, and the engine shows its work either way.

Does it give me the duties and trade measures, or only the ten digits?

Both. The engine returns the code, then the measures that attach to it for the origin you enter: the third-country duty, any anti-dumping or countervailing duty, suspensions, and quotas. You see the rate you would declare on entry, not the heading on its own.

Does Aimport check existing binding tariff rulings?

Yes. It searches the EBTI database for binding rulings on goods like yours and shows them next to the result. You see how customs classified comparable products before you commit to a heading, and you spot a conflict before an officer does.

How does it handle parts, sets, and composite goods?

The engine works through GRI 2 to 4 in order: unfinished and disassembled articles under GRI 2(a), composite goods and sets by their essential character under GRI 3(b), and the tie-break under GRI 3(c). The trail names the rule that settled the case, so you can defend the call.

Who carries responsibility for the final classification?

You do. The declarant signs the declaration and owns the result. Aimport gives you the reasoning to check and the trail to defend, and it files nothing on your behalf.
